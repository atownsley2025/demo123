ZeroSkid: Detecting Wet Roads with Deep Learning

ZeroSkid is a proof-of-concept project that uses computer vision to tell whether a road surface is wet or dry. The idea connects to a larger safety concept: if a car can recognize dangerous wet conditions in real time, it can warn the driver or adjust driving behavior to reduce the chance of hydroplaning.

The Problem

Wet roads are a big safety issue, especially when drivers do not realize how slippery the surface actually is. Many crashes happen because the road looks “normal” until a car hits a slick patch of water and loses traction. Most current systems focus on speed or distance to other cars, but do not really look at the road surface itself.

My project focuses on a simpler but important sub-problem: can we train a model to visually tell the difference between a wet road and a dry road using images?

Motivation

The motivation behind ZeroSkid is to explore how AI could help prevent accidents before they happen. If a model can recognize wet conditions from a camera feed, it could be part of a system that:

  • Warns the driver when the road is potentially unsafe
  • Adjusts cruise control or braking behavior on wet surfaces
  • Helps insurance and safety systems better understand driving conditions

This project is not a full product yet. It is a small, controlled version of the idea that focuses on image classification as a first step toward smarter road-condition awareness.

Dataset Overview

For this project, I created a custom dataset with two classes: wet and dry roads. Each image shows a road surface and is labeled based on its condition. The images are stored in folders like train/wet, train/dry, test/wet, and test/dry. All images are resized to 224×224 pixels and normalized so they can be used with AlexNet.

The dataset is fairly small, which makes training a bit noisy, but it is realistic for a student project and still gives the model enough information to learn meaningful patterns between wet and dry road surfaces.
